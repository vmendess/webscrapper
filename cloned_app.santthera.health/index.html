Scientific Evidence for Key Ingredients (Memory & Cognitive Support)

The studies below suggest these ingredients may help support healthy brain function, memory retention, and cognitive performance. Individual results may vary.

Bacopa Monnieri Extract
  • Meta-analysis: Bacopa monnieri significantly improves attention, cognitive processing, and working memory. PubMed (2014)
  • Systematic review: Evidence supports Bacopa's role in enhancing memory acquisition and retention in healthy adults. PubMed (2017)
  • Clinical trial: 12-week supplementation improved memory consolidation and reduced anxiety. PubMed (2008)
Ginkgo Biloba Extract
  • Meta-analysis of RCTs: Ginkgo biloba improves cognitive function and activities of daily living in dementia patients. PubMed (2015)
  • Systematic review: Evidence for enhanced memory and processing speed in healthy individuals. PubMed (2012)
  • Clinical study: Ginkgo extract improves cerebral blood flow and cognitive performance. PubMed (2001)
Phosphatidylserine
  • Double-blind trial: Phosphatidylserine supplementation improved memory and cognitive function in elderly subjects. PubMed (1991)
  • Review: Phosphatidylserine plays a key role in neurotransmitter release and synaptic function. PubMed (2015)
Lion's Mane Mushroom (Hericium erinaceus)
  • Clinical trial: Lion's Mane improved cognitive function in older adults with mild cognitive impairment over 16 weeks. PubMed (2009)
  • Review: Hericenones and erinacines from Lion's Mane stimulate nerve growth factor (NGF) synthesis. PubMed (2013)
Omega-3 Fatty Acids (DHA)
  • Meta-analysis: DHA supplementation associated with improved memory and cognitive function. PubMed (2012)
  • Review: DHA is essential for brain development and maintaining neuronal membrane integrity. PubMed (2016)
  • Clinical study: Higher DHA levels correlate with better episodic memory performance in aging adults. PubMed (2010)
Huperzine A
  • Meta-analysis: Huperzine A demonstrates beneficial effects on cognitive function in Alzheimer's disease patients. PubMed (2008)
  • Review: Acts as a potent acetylcholinesterase inhibitor, enhancing acetylcholine levels in the brain. PubMed (2013)
Vitamin B Complex (B6, B9, B12)
  • Meta-analysis: B vitamin supplementation slows cognitive decline and brain atrophy in elderly with elevated homocysteine. PubMed (2010)
  • Review: B vitamins are essential cofactors for neurotransmitter synthesis and myelin formation. PubMed (2016)
  • Clinical trial: Combined B vitamin supplementation preserved cognitive function over 2 years in older adults. PubMed (2013)
  • Note: Deficiency in B12 is linked to accelerated cognitive decline and neurological issues. PubMed (2008)
